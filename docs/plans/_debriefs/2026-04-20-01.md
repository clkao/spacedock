The result: tests that pass on behaviors we didn't want (inline-Path-B collapse in teams mode) and fail on behaviors we did want (the trajectory the prompt-contract actually specifies). Cycle-7's structural fix was to **pick ONE trajectory per test and assert on its specific cycle-7 primitives** (per-dispatch close anchors, on-disk evidence, teams-mode pin). Cycle-8 extended the principle with structural pivots (on-disk `ECHO: ping` assertion instead of FO-stream grep). Cycle-10/12 refined further (case-insensitive match because the primitive is the dispatch-close, not the ensign's paraphrase of its description). Pattern: tests that accept multiple trajectories hide flakes in their OR-chains; tight trajectory-pinned tests catch flakes as real signal AND drive FO discipline improvements.
